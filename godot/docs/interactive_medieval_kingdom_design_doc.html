Project Design Document

Pre-Production (v0.1) Godot 4.x / Unreal 5 Britain 1200–1300 AD

1. Vision & Pillars

A medieval political simulation where the player governs as a monarch or noble, navigating a living web of feudal relationships, dynastic intrigue, economic management, and military conflict across 13th century Britain.

Emergent Political Drama

No scripted story. Every rebellion, betrayal, and alliance emerges from the simulation. The game generates its own historical drama through the interaction of systems.

The Gaussian Kingdom

Every major system operates on a normal distribution. The goal is to keep systems near their optimal centre. Too much or too little royal power triggers crisis.

Knowledge as Power

No omniscient vision. Intelligence is earned through espionage, diplomacy, and economic presence. Redacted family trees create strategic tension.

Living History

The game writes its own chronicle. Every character, battle, and betrayal is recorded. A Bard (LLM) can narrate any character's arc.

2. Engine & Technology

Primary Engine: Godot 4

  • 2D first-class — the campaign map, region polygons, UI panels are trivial compared to UE5's 2D overhead.
  • Node architecture mirrors feudal hierarchy — a County node contains Barony child nodes. The scene tree IS the political map.
  • GDScript maps directly to designed systems — graph traversal, Gaussian sampling, and Voronoi data integrate cleanly.

Renderer Configuration

Use the Compatibility renderer (OpenGL 3.3) for the campaign layer. Do not use Forward+ (D3D12) for 2D. Set in project.godot:

[rendering]
renderer/rendering_method="gl_compatibility"

4. Political Titles & Appointments

Titles are not fixed — they are granted, revoked, contested, and inherited. Each title carries land, income, military obligations, and political weight.

TitleTierNotes
Emperor0Holy Roman Empire only. "First among equals" over kings
King / Queen0Supreme ruler of a kingdom
Duke / Duchess1Ruler of a duchy. Often a royal relative
Earl / Count1Ruler of a county or shire. Justice and governance
Baron / Baroness2Foundational landed nobility. Controls a manor
Knight / Chevalier3Holds a fief in exchange for military service

Appointment Mechanics

  • Licence to Crenellate: Royal document required to build battlements. Building without one is rebellion.
  • Great Offices of State: Lord High Steward, Chancellor, Treasurer. Grants economic/military bonuses.
  • Scutage: Money paid instead of knight service. Legally permitted but politically telling.

6. The Gaussian Design Philosophy

Every major system is modelled as a normal distribution. The player's goal is not to maximise any single metric but to keep multiple systems near their optimal centre simultaneously.

Each system has:
  Mean     = the stable, optimal state ("Fun Zone")
  StdDev   = how volatile the system is
  Current  = where it sits right now

Feudal Balance

Left: Mass baronial rebellion (Crown too powerful).
Right: Kingdom fragments (Barons too powerful).
Center: Mutual respect.

Piety Balance

Left: Excommunication, peasant unrest (Too secular).
Right: Church owns untaxable land (Too religious).
Center: Funded & respected.

7. The Feudal Node Graph

The political world is modelled as a weighted directed graph. Every lord is a node. Every relationship is a weighted edge. When a child node's influence exceeds its parent's, a conflict event triggers (The Displacement Mechanic).

Interactive: Drag the nodes below to explore the political network structure.

The Blackfyre Scenario

Bastards accumulate influence independently. If a bastard's influence approaches their half-sibling's and they have a patron, they can assert a claim, triggering a cascade from whispers to full civil war.

10. Economy System

Peasants are not individual characters. They are an aggregate population number per region that drives economic emergence.

Population threshold → Merchants spawn
Merchants threshold  → Guild spawns
Guild income feeds   → Church tithe
Church tithe         → Cathedral or Monastery spawns
Cathedral            → Bishop NPC character created

The Wool-Cloth Dynamic

England produces raw wool. Flanders processes it and sells it back at triple the price. Controlling Calais controls the wool staple — whoever controls Flanders controls the most profitable trade route in Northern Europe.

11 & 12. Faith & Dynasty Systems

The Church Power

  • Owns 25–30% of all land in 13th century England.
  • Tithe: 10% of all produce goes to Church.
  • The Crusade Release Valve: Exports surplus younger sons (solves dynastic overpopulation), boosts piety, but costs economy -15% for the duration.

Noble Attributes

  • Martial (1-10): Battle effectiveness.
  • Diplomacy (1-10): Alliance formation.
  • Stewardship (1-10): Tax efficiency.
  • Intrigue (1-10): Spy operations.
  • Piety (1-10): Crusade effectiveness.

15. AI Personality System

Every NPC is a blend of two archetypes with one dominant. The combination produces distinct decision-making patterns through Gaussian sampling.

Example NPC Blends

The Warmonger (Expansionist + Warrior) Seeks maximum territory and military glory. Constantly challenges peers and marches armies.
The Shadow Bishop (Schemer + Pious) Uses church influence to eliminate rivals indirectly. Places spies in monasteries.
The True Steward (Loyalist + Builder) Supports current power by generating massive economic growth and avoiding conflict.

16 & 17. Battles & Sieges (Phase 3)

Unreal Engine 5 Battles

Real-time battles integrate UE5's Mass Entity system (targeting 10,000+ agents). Knights hold ranks, infantry form walls, and morale dictates victory.

Siege Mechanics (The Golden Age of Castles)

Castles evolve from Motte & Bailey to Concentric fortresses. Trebuchets are built on-site over multiple turns and feature varying Gaussian accuracies per shot. Sieges track garrison provisions—starving them out is viable.
